Marine took a few quick, panting exhalations. She had come to an undersea cave, a pocket of air - a little air - and so she had untied and removed her headband, dispelling the magicked bubble of air around her. Exhausted, she let it fall out of her hand and onto the damp cave floor.

Before her, visible through her grayed vision, her eyes adjusting to the cave’s darkness, was something akin to a flower garden. It was a decorated mass of flowering sea algae, numerous in colors, appearing to be pulsing slightly with life. She had never seen anything like it, in all her days diving beneath the waves.

“Whoa,” she felt herself let out.

HELLO

A voice unlike any she had heard spoke to her in perfect clarity. Not out loud - inside her mind. She had heard of such things, but it was unusual, not to mention unexpected, to feel for the first time.

Puzzled, she beheld the wriggling seaweed like flowered mass before her. Its petals seemed to shake. Was this thing living? Talking to her?

“Hello?” she tentatively whispered.

YES HELLO THERE

Despite the “creature’s” friendly greeting, she felt her hand drift to the hilt of her blade instinctually. Though it being a plant, she would’ve been better off with fire.

I MEAN YOU NO HARM

Again, the voice pounded through her head, at what felt like crushing volume. It was so uniquely close. It felt a bit like she had lost control of her mind.

“What are you?”

Marine beheld the thing, leaning her head forward and taking it in –

IT’S COMPLICATED

– she noticed particulates drifting through the air, almost imperceptible but visible as clusters in motion, perhaps, lightly green in color, the flowers seeming to emanate and be surrounded by them –

BUT AS I SAID, I MEAN YOU NO HARM

She swatted in front of her face. She said, talking at the general flowers before her,

“What is this stuff? Is it dangerous to me?”

She looked for her handkerchief she had left on the ground in the dark.

NO, WORRY NOT

THESE ARE PURELY COMMUNICATIVE SPORES SPREAD BETWEEN MY SPECIES

“Communicative?” she pondered. “Is that how you’re talking to me? Invading my mind?” She brushed at her ear.

NO I ALSO HAVE INNATE TELEPATHY

THE SPORES ARE ONLY FOR INTERACTING WITH OTHER MEMBERS OF MY SPECIES

She took pause at this. Could she trust whatever this thing is? She didn’t want to die, forgotten in a sea cave after inhaling spores from a seemingly trustworthy clump of seaweed.

She took hold of her handkerchief, tying it again around her head in a quick and simple double knot. It created an air bubble around her head.

“Just to be safe, alright, I’m doing this,” although she felt she must have already inhaled some of the spores. ‘There are dangers beneath the sea not meant for our understanding,’ she remembered of the old sea hag who had traded her this thing.

NO PROBLEM

THOUGH I ASSURE YOU THE SPORES ARE ONLY A MINOR NUISANCE TO YOUR SPECIES

MATTER THAT IS INARTICULATE AND MEANINGLESS TO YOU, LIKE POLLEN

“What’s pollen,” she wanted to ask, but felt like she shouldn’t bother. “So,” she asked instead, “what are you? Your species, I mean?”

WE HAVE STUDIED YOUR TONGUE EXTENSIVELY BUT I HAVE FOUND NO EXACT WORD

WE ARE FUNGI THAT DWELL THROUGHOUT YOUR WORLD, CONNECTED THROUGH TENDRILS, PHYSICAL PATHWAYS LIKE YOU SEE BEFORE YOU

WE COMMUNICATE WITH EACH OTHER THROUGH THESE PATHWAYS

AND MANIFEST AS CLUSTERS OF FLOWERS THAT PRODUCE AND DISPERSE OUR PARTICULATE THOUGHT PATTERNS

AND SO ON

“Whoa, slow down,” she said, half out of confusion, and half that the loud pulsing in her head as this thing communicated with her was overwhelming her, half-making her a bit nauseous.

“Sorry, uh, what’s your name…?”

I HAVE NONE

I HAVE NO INDIVIDUAL IDENTITY

I AM MERELY A NERVE CENTER OF ONE COLLECTIVE BEING

“Okay, well, ‘nerve center’... is it possible for you to ‘talk’ a bit quieter?”

NO

Her head throbbed. “That’s okay… you said something about ‘thought patterns’? These particles are thoughts?”

YES

OBSERVATIONS AND MUSINGS

SOMETIMES QUESTIONS, CONVERSATIONS

BETWEEN THE CLUSTERS

LIKE ME

She beheld the odd cloud of dust before her. How could something so small and invisible be anything like that? She tried to catch some in her hand, get it to cling to her skin somehow. She watched it merely disperse back through the air. Like light dust, but almost like it had a mind of its own.

PRODUCED BIOMATTER

IT IS MARKED WITH OUR THOUGHT PATTERNS

LIKE A DOG MARKS A TREE

OR LIKE HOW A SONG IS WRITTEN ON A PARCHMENT SCROLL

She was still befuddled by these dust motes. “How do you know all this? Know so much, I mean. About dogs.”

WE COLLECT AND DISPERSE KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS

THROUGH THIS MATTER

“Observe? How do you observe?”

She looked for clues in the tendril of green, tendrilled limbs and flowers before her. It felt more like a collection of plants than any living organism. She didn’t want to step on it.

THE FLOWERS CONTAIN SENSORY OBSERVERS

LIKE EYES

Marine squinted at the flowers.

BUT TOO SMALL FOR YOU TO REGARD THEM AS SUCH

“Oh.” The flower appeared just like almost any other flower. She wondered if other plants could “see”. “So you see stuff, you tell other people about it through the dust spores?”

JUST OTHERS LIKE ME

THE SPORES ARE MEANINGLESS TO “PEOPLE”

Marine swatted for more spores. She felt otherwise, somehow.

I WAS BORN IN THIS CAVE

I SEE, OBSERVE VERY LITTLE OF NOTE

AND COMMUNICATE VERY LITTLE AS A RESULT

“Oh,” Marine offered, “I’m sorry.”

DON’T BE

I RECEIVE PLENTY FROM THE TRANSMISSIONS OF OTHERS

THEIR THOUGHTS, THEIR FEELINGS, THEIR OBSERVATIONS

AND THROUGH THIS I AM CONNECTED TO THE WORLD

“Wow,” Marine said. She was almost envious of him, this strange flowered clump of sea moss. “These transmissions… tell me about them.”

YOU CAN LISTEN TO A FEW

I WILL TRANSLATE THEM FOR YOU

She became excited, like she was cracking open a chest, or corking open a message in a bottle. “Tell me, tell me!”

OKAY

THIS ONE SAYS

“RACCOON KING DEAD TODAY. NOT MOURNED BY HIS FAMILY MUCH”

“What?” Marine was surprised.

THIS IS IN REFERENCE TO AN EARLIER SERIES OF DISPATCHES ABOUT A CLAN OF RACCOONS WHO HAD APPOINTED A SORT OF LEADER FIGURE

WORRY NOT. I WILL READ ANOTHER

 “ONE TWO ONE ONE ONE TWO ONE ONE ONE ONE TWO ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE ONE”

“What,” Marine said, recoiling at the loud and repetitive banging in her head, “stop doing that. What is that?”

A TRANSMISSION, FROM A FARAWAY NODE

EACH HAS THEIR OWN PERSONALITY OF SORTS

SOME COME WITH LITTLE EXPLANATION

I HAVE BEEN GETTING TRANSMISSIONS WITH PATTERNS OF NUMBERS IN THEM FOR AS LONG AS I CAN REMEMBER

Memory, she thought. “That’s strange. Do you collect these?”

NO I CONSUME THEM TO RECEIVE THEM

“Oh,” she said. That only added further to the mystery. She thought about dispelling the bubble around her mouth and trying to inhale as many of the spores as possible, but this felt contrary to her earlier, perhaps wiser caution. Could still be toxic, she thought, but she was curious…

THEY ALSO PROVIDE NUTRITION

I AM PARTICULARLY DEPENDENT ON THEM, NOT ONLY FOR KNOWLEDGE

BUT BECAUSE THIS CAVE RECEIVES NO LIGHT

“Oh?” Even with her limited knowledge of flora (and fauna), she knew mushrooms typically grew in dark places. “You need sunlight?”

OR THE ENERGY EXTRACTED FROM IT, DISPERSED IN SPORES

“Right.”

Half-absentmindedly, she lit a small, candle-like flame that floated from the palm of her hand.

“Light… is this helpful for you?”

NO

BUT IT IS INTERESTING

“I’ll keep it going, then,” she said, giving a light smile. She looked around the cave, now faintly illuminated from her palm. “How are you getting these spores, anyway? Through the air?” Other than the way she came in, a small gate into the ocean above like an ocular, it appeared entirely like a tiny, closed off cavern.

CONNECTED, LIKE I SAID, TO THE OTHERS

A TENDRIL RUNS OUT FROM ME TO THE NEXT AND SO ON

LIKE A ROAD, THE “SPORES” TRAVEL INSIDE THIS, AND ARE RELEASED INTO THE AIR BRIEFLY FROM MY ORIFICES BEFORE I CONSUME THEM

This whole explanation, while helpful, grossed her out a little, though she had difficulty explaining why. Maybe the fact that this creature knew what “orifices” were.

THIS ROAD GOES TWO WAYS

IN RETURN, I DEPOSIT WHAT ENERGY I ABSORB BACK INTO THE SYSTEM

“Interesting,” she said, “like, um… bio-lumin– um, mutual codependence.”

YES

THOUGH WE ARE ALL ONE ORGANISM

IN YOUR TERMS

She paused. She wondered how much of this “thing” there really was. She had only imagined the two nodes that had sent the messages, but she supposed she had no way of knowing how long this thing stretched.

THIS IS THE LONGEST ANYONE HAS STOPPED HERE

“Really? You’ve never had any other company?”

Marine thought reflexively of the people that had entered, and left, her life. She felt grateful.

YOUNG FISH, SEPARATED FROM THEIR SCHOOLS, HAVE SOMETIMES WANDERED INTO THIS CAVE

TO DIE HELPLESS ON ITS FLOOR

THEY DECOMPOSE AND ROT BEFORE MY “EYES”

AND I CONSUME WHAT MATTER THEY LEAVE BEHIND

“Oh.” Marine gazed back through the portal to the sea.

I TRY TO OFFER WHAT SOLACE I CAN TO THEM

BUT THEY ARE POOR CONVERSATIONALISTS

She felt pity for the collection of plant matter. Such a lonely existence it lived, though she didn’t want to voice this thought. Everything was relative, after all.

I HAD ONE OTHER VISITOR BUT HE DIDN’T STAY LONG

AN “ELVEN” ONE

“Oh?” Marine had not given thought to others, like her, wandering beneath the sea, despite its vastness, its connection to everything. That another had been where she stood.

YES

HE RODE A DOLPHIN

Marine’s yellowed eyes sparkled in astonishment at this information. Druids were so lucky…

HE CAME IN, BEHELD ME, AND TOOK A CLIPPING OF ME, SEALING IT IN A SMALL JAR

“Ouch,” Marine winced. “Without asking?”

WITHOUT ASKING

BUT I DO NOT FEEL PAIN

AND GREW THE FLOWER BACK WITHIN A FEW DAYS

“Oh.” She thought of thinking a flower or perhaps a petal for herself as a souvenir, but realized she wouldn’t know what to do with it.

PERHAPS YOU SHOULD GO TOO SOON

THERE IS MUCH TO SEE IN THE SURFACE WORLD

She thought, but she had so much left to ask this thing.

“Do you know, like, where you came from? Where you ‘originate’?”

FROM THE STARS

“From the stars?”

THAT IS ALL WE KNOW

PASSED DOWN TO US FROM UNREMEMBERED TIMES

She thought about this, but didn’t know what to make of it. Could not even be true, but rather the religion and mythos of … fungi. She supposed all of Creation was made “from the stars”, according to the church.

“But where do you… how big are you? Like, do you know where you stop? Can you feel the other nodes?”

NO

THIS CLUMP OF MASS YOU SEE BEFORE YOU IS LIKE OUR “NERVE CENTER”, OR BRAIN

I CANNOT FEEL BEYOND THAT

I ONLY KNOW OF WHAT I RECIEVE

“The transmissions,” she said, coming to some minor understanding of the creature.

I KNOW WE ARE ON THE SURFACE

IN SWAMPS, IN WOODS, IN FORGOTTEN CAVES LIKE THIS ONE

IN THE DEPTHS OF FORGOTTEN WELLS

IN THE DUNGEONS OF CASTLES

AND I KNOW WE ARE FAR, FAR BELOW AS WELL

AN EXTENSIVE NETWORK OF NODES COMMUNICATE EXCLUSIVELY BETWEEN EACH OTHER

THEIR TRANSMISSIONS NEVER ESCAPING THEIR OWN CLUSTER

“Woah,” she remarked, “How do you know about those guys?”

I EXAGGERATED SLIGHTLY

THEIR TRANSMISSIONS REACH OTHERS, WHO SPREAD WORD OF THEIR ACTIVITIES OUTSIDE AND SO ON

Gossip, in other words, she thought.

BUT THIS COLLECTIVE ITSELF EMITS UNUSUAL MATTER

A BIT LIKE THEY “SPEAK IN THEIR OWN TONGUE”

BARELY DECIPHERABLE BY SURFACERS, OR THOSE LIKE ME WHO HAVE ADAPTED TO COMMON LONG AGO

THEY MAY HAVE MUTATED BEYOND OUR UNDERSTANDING COMPLETELY

“Huh,” she said, having not even considered why the plant was able to speak Common. She assumed it was some complex mind translation.

She reached out, somehow compelled to touch the vines of the creature, to feel it, without thinking. It was slightly wet and slimy, and beyond that, it felt texturally as though it was lightly haired. “Sorry,” she said, “can you feel that?”

NO

“Oh, alright.” She continued feeling the plant. Down, and then up, she could feel it was a bit pointed, like the scales of her own skin, but much smaller, more gentle. It was strangely pleasant. It calmed her, for a moment.

She supposed she did need to leave eventually. A ship awaited her return. But she felt bad leaving the creature here, to be forgotten beneath the waves.

She pulled her lute from its shoulder sling to her front, and plucked it. It was dry, thanks to the air bubble (although it had gotten wet countless times before).

She felt its engravings, “Before I go, would you like to hear a song?”

A SONG

WHAT IS THAT

She thought. “It’s a bit like your transmissions. I play them, with this thing, though. Sound, you know?”

YES

She smiled, and began tuning the lute. She supposed this thing wanted to know more about the surface.

She began playing the most popular song she knew,

“Today

Is gonna be the day

That they’re gonna throw it back to you

By now

You’ve surely somehow”

STOP

The strings of the lute twanged and reverberated against her fingers as she was caught off guard by the sudden head intrusion. “What?”

SORRY

COULD YOU PLAY SOMETHING ELSE

“Like what? Any requests?”

YOU PLAY THESE SONGS, RIGHT

“Yes,” she said, with a note of pride.

IS THERE SOMETHING YOU’VE WRITTEN

She sighed. There was, but not recently… things had been too busy. Life. Songwriting had gotten away from her.

SOMETHING MORE LIKE YOU

She smiled. She almost could’ve been tricked into thinking this seaweed was flirting with her.

“Quite smooth of you,” she said, “Maybe…”

“Here, I haven’t done this in a while, so forgive the results, but I can improvise something, perhaps…”

She felt for a few chords. They were familiar in her hands, but sounded unfamiliar in the acoustics of the sea-cave. She closed her eyes, and began, in a lilting and casual tone, as she strummed along a four-chord melody,

“Beneath the waves

The emerald and gray

The sea-sleeper hides away

In his hideaway, with naught much to say,

Supping on fish bone and spores,

Even in a dark room, his flowers they bloom

Their beauty all hidd’n away

In cave under wave

The sea-sleeper is

Through letters he lives,

But none does he give

For he observes naught

But fish and they die

But much does he know

Wisened, does he grow

For words from far above

From those he doth love,

Bring the sea-sleeper his meal

Under ‘neath the waves

Neath the water he grows

A being like no other

His brothers only know

Hidden in cavern away from the daylight,

‘Tis where the sea-sleeper sleeps tonight”

She took a deep exhalation, finishing the song.

THAT WAS NICE

I DON’T SLEEP THOUGH

“Can’t win ‘em all, I guess. Goodnight, sea sleeper.” She untied the bow around her head and redid it around her wrist, forming an air bubble around 5 feet wide around her body.

GOODNIGHT
